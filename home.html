Project Proposal

BEYOND
THE CHAT

Every student loves AI, but talking to AI isn't learning from AI.

Current AI tools are like learning from a dictionary—they give you definitions, but they don't teach you the language. We are building the teacher.

The Student Reality

Chat is a mess. Learning needs a map.

The "Memory" Problem

Knowledge is scattered across 50 different chat threads. Finding a specific explanation from last week is nearly impossible. Students lose interest when they lose their progress.

The "Starting" Problem

If you don't know what to ask, chat is useless. Students get confused and stop. We provide the "First Step" so they never have to guess what to learn next.

The Xerox Analogy

Stop paying for the same answer twice.

When 50 students ask the same question, current AI "prints" a new answer for each. It's expensive and slow.

Traditional AI (The Print Shop)
50x

Costly, redundant, and unpredictable.

Project Level (The Xerox)
1x

Generate once, serve 50,000 times for free.

The Economics

₹4.20 per student, per year.

This is cheaper than printing a physical textbook and significantly more effective than a static PDF.

Scenario Token Usage Cost Pattern
Chat-Based: 100 students ask the same question ~100,000 Pay per student (Scales poorly)
Project Level: Generate once, serve 100 students ~1,000 Pay once (Scales infinitely)

At 70,000 students, we move from burning cash to sustainable scaling.

The Solution

Teaching AI, not Talking AI.

New Learning Platform

Proactive teaching. It tells the student: "Here is what you need to know today." It builds foundations before they get confused.

TAP Buddy

The reactive partner. When a student has a specific doubt about the lesson, TAP Buddy answers. Textbook meets Tutor.

Research-Based Impact

Science, not just software.

Spaced Practice

Retention improves by 30-50% when students review concepts at set intervals. We automate this.

Micro-Lessons

5-10 minute "bursts" of learning reduce cognitive load and build daily habits that actually stick.

You can't build a house by piling bricks in a heap; you need a blueprint. We are that blueprint.
